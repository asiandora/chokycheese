Baking the Perfect Hokkaido Cheese Tarts

The road to finding the secret recipe.

To kick off this project, we did some research on what makes a Hokkaido Cheese Tart so perfect so we can bake the exact same ones.

What makes them so delicious?

1) Pastry Texture

The pastry is so crispy in contrast to the custard, and it smells so amazing. The pastry does not fall apart easily.

2) Pastry Flavour

It’s not so buttery that the taste takes over the custard. It has the perfect pastry and custard ratio.

3) Custard Texture

The custard is gooey, light and smooth. And the top egg wash holds it all in.

4) Custard Flavour

The cheese flavours is what really blew my mind. I could not identify the cheeses in the custard. But I knew they definitely used Japanese branded cheeses and milk.

5) HOT AND FRESH!!!

The story behind the molten cheese tarts was that the seller of BAKE cheese tart (the brand) ran out packing boxes so he started selling the tart hot out of the oven on the trays still. The customers started rolling in and that’s how he rose to fame.

First Recipe Experiment!

Our moment of doubt.

CHOKY SENIOR BAKED HER VERY FIRST CHOKY CHEESE TARTS!!

And failed miserably...

Rewind 4 hours before:

Before I started, I did loads of research on potential CHOKY CHEESE TART recipes. Watched a couple videos (In English, Thai, and Chinese hehehehhe), read a few articles and realised…

There is not a single recipe online that closely resembles the famous Hokkaido cheese tarts. None.

But I needed to start somewhere. I finally chose 4 recipes which you can find here. Maybe the flavour will be similar, but it’s looking like I will have to experiment a lot.

I decided to try Recipe 2 first, because it had the most cheese varieties therefore seemed the hardest (I was to find out that I was wrong). I sourced all my ingredients on Ocado (an online UK supermarket) which sources their groceries from loads of supermarkets.

To make the tart pastry

The ingredients were pretty simple…

Butter, flour, sugar, vanilla, egg yolk. The ingredients chosen to create a perfect ball of pastry dough.

Smooth as a baby’s bum… (wrong analogy??)

And a perfectly lined tray of tart pastry cups to store in the fridge.

I know I know, there’s one that’s cracked.

And to make the cheese custard

I used a variety of cheeses — cheddar, parmesan, cream cheese, cheese cubes and mascarpone. With the double boiler method, I mixed all the cheeses with full cream milk and a little bit of cornstarch and lemon.

The Heads

By Clara Chok

What makes a good logo? Well, I don’t know. This was my first time trying to design one and boy was it tough. As with all designs, I began this logo journey with some initial ideas. Mistakes were made and I drew a page worth of ideas under the wrong impression that we were making egg tarts. However, this did result in some adorable little chick designs :)

Alright, moving on. Having the logo be based on our heads was an idea that first came to me when I tried to draw my sister as an egg. (See, the egg tart mistake did come in handy.) However, as you can see she just looked extremely obese. Totally an accident. But we did agree that because this was such a personal brand between the two of us, it would be a good idea to use our heads as our logo. Let the minimalist head designs begin…

Here is a real life photo of us so you all know what I was designing from:

When both your parents are taking photos so you don’t know which camera to look at

I experimented with what I perceived to be the most prominent features on our faces, and for Chok senior, that was her glasses. As for myself, my thick sausage lips. Also, I have a strong personal opinion that my sister has a rather structured squarish head, whilst my head is basically just a sphere. Designs were sketched. Adorable little chicks tried to make it in but were brutally dismissed by a certain co-founder. Heads were shaped. A logo was slowly starting to emerge, but it needed a few tweaks. There was a complaint from Chok Sr. that a sense of beauty and the geek was coming through, and if she had to look nerdy, then my lips had to be comically thick.

“HAHAHA I tried to draw you as an egg but you just look fat.”

“This kinda works because you have a square head and I have a circular one”
